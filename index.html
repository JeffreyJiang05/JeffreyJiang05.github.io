What is an Algae Bloom?

Algae blooms are unnatural overproduction of algae population that build up in various water sources. They pollute water sources and can be harmful and toxic. They are easy to spot!

Some Numbers

In a study that tracked algal blooms all over the world from 2003 to 2020 found that:

Area of algal bloom increased by 1.5 million square miles

Algal blooms increased ~13% in size

Frequency of algal blooms increased by ~59%

So what is the problem?

Algae blooms pose a threat through eutrophication, contaminating water supplies, and decreasing biodiversity.
